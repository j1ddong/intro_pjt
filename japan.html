JAPAN

Language
The Japanese language is a complex system with Kanji, Hiragana, and Katakana scripts. It emphasizes politeness, has SOV sentence structure, and distinct levels of formality. Cultural expressions and honorifics are significant, and pitch accent affects meaning. Learning Japanese offers insights into rich cultural nuances and societal values.
Historical Significance
Japan has a complex history marked by various periods, including feudal eras, samurai culture, and periods of isolation from the outside world. The Meiji Restoration in the 19th century brought about modernization and opened Japan to global influences.
